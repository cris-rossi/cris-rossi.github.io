Hi! I am Cris, a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Neuroscience at Johns Hopkins University.

My research interests lie at the intersection of behavioral neuroscience and cognitive social psychology. I am interested in understanding how our embodied perception of self shapes our actions, learning, identity and social cognition.

I received my Master's degree in Biomedical Engineering (1st Hons) from Imperial College London (London, UK), and completed my Ph.D. training in Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins University (Baltimore, USA). As a postdoctoral researcher, I work with Dr. Amy Bastian to study how the perceptual experience of one’s own body shapes the way we move in different environments.

As I transition from neuroscience towards cognitive psychology, I hope to repurpose my expertise in self-perception and expand towards more complex cognitive and social processes, in particular through the lens of diverse identities.
