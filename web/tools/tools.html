MovementVR

MovementVR is a platform to create motor control and learning experiments in immersive Virtual Reality without any coding.

I began using VR during the pandemic – like many others, I was hoping to run experiments remotely by shipping VR headsets.

I quickly realized that VR has enormous potential not only because it is portable, but because it can make experiments more like the real world. So why isn’t this tool more commonly used in labs during normal times?

Just as quickly, I discovered how challenging it is to design realistic studies in VR. Over the next few years – alongside other projects – I worked on developing MovementVR: an open-source, code-free tool designed to make building and customizing realistic motor tasks simple and accessible. Visit movementvr.github.io.
